Project Overview

Naija Groups was developed as part of a collaborative effort to enhance communication and resource sharing among university students in Nigeria. The platform addresses the fragmented nature of student communications by providing a centralized hub for announcements, study materials, and real-time interactions.

The Challenge

University students faced significant challenges in coordinating group activities, sharing resources, and staying updated with important announcements. Traditional communication methods like WhatsApp groups became cluttered and disorganized, making it difficult to find important information.

The goal was to create a unified platform that would streamline all student communications while maintaining performance and security standards.

Key Learnings

This project taught me the importance of scalable architecture when building platforms for community use. Managing real-time data flow while maintaining performance required careful optimization of database queries and WebSocket connections.

Implementing proper security measures from the start proved crucial for gaining user trust and protecting sensitive student information.

Working in a team environment enhanced my collaboration skills and taught me the value of clear communication and documentation in group projects.
